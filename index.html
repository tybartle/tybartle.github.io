Cow Chips 4 Charity

August 2019 - Present

This application is still currently under development by myself and five other students. As part of our Senior Design course at ISU we have been professionally tasked by a client to create this project and to have the completed product by May 2020. I personally am the Game Team Leader for our team, in charge of making sure the game portion of the project gets completed successfully.

Cow Chips 4 Charity is a web-based game with the purpose of fundraising for the Boo Radley Foundation - an organization dedicated to promoting and raising money for medical research into diseases that affect both humans and our companion animals. The game is a variation of Bingo and will be hosted on a website that my team redesigned. We also are creating backend data analytics for admin users to be able to see statistics about the various games played and donations received.

Java, Vue.js, Node.js, Express, MongoDB, Heroku

WITWAY
(Where In The World Are You)

August - December 2018

This application was created by myself and three other students for a computer science class. At the end of the semester our group received an Honorable Mention meaning we were in the top 10 best projects out of the 50 total groups.

WITWAY is an Android mobile application created for travellers as an easy way to plan a trip. The user would select their desired location and interests and WITWAY would generate a list of venues and activities that fit their selections for them or their group that they can create in the app. WITWAY can also show the routes to the places in the user's schedule and allows them to rate the places they have gone.

Java, XML, Android Studio, Springboot, Volley, Google Maps API, YelpFusion

Util3D

January - May 2018

I created this application along with a team of seven other students for a computer science class. At the end of the semester our project was voted 3rd out of 11 teams by our classmates, TAs, and professors.

Util3D is an Android application designed to assist field technicians in the process of mapping utility lines (water, gas, electric, sewage, etc.). Through interaction with Google Maps, users can create “projects” in order to plan out new utility lines or view/edit existing utility lines. By creating these utility line projects, field technicians can essentially create a massive collaborative archive for maintenance purposes.

Java, XML, Android Studio, Google Firebase, Google Maps API
